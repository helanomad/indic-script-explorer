Brahmi is one of the earliest writing systems of South Asia and the ancestor of most Indic scripts.

This interactive Brahmi Alphabet chart is part of the Indic Script Explorer by Hela Nomad. It presents the core letters and signs of the Brahmi writing system, with Romanization and pronunciation.

Select any letter to hear its sound and visualize how it is written, stroke by stroke.

Script Notes: Historical script, modern font

Brahmi is an ancient script with many regional and chronological forms.
The glyphs shown here use "Noto Sans Brahmi", a modern Unicode
typeface designed for digital representation.

These forms are a standardized scholarly reconstruction and are not tied to a
single century or region.

Vowel length note: In the Brahmi script tradition, the vowels e and o
are inherently long. There are no separate independent letters for short
e/o versus long ƒì/≈ç. Modern Romanizations may show this distinction,
but it is not encoded in Brahmi orthography.
